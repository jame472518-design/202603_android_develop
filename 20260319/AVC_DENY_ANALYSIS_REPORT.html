🔍 Root Cause Analysis

  • Qualcomm vendor HAL services lack SELinux policy: New BSP components (vendor_hal_perf2_service, vendor_hal_qspmhal_default, vendor_nfc_vendor_data_file) were introduced without corresponding .te rules in device/<vendor>/<device>/sepolicy/.
  • hal_camera_default policy too narrow: Camera HAL performs a full property scan (__system_property_foreach), likely from Qualcomm camera library. Policy only grants access to a small subset. Needs either broader get_prop macros or camera lib fix.
  • radio/vendor_init property rules missing: RIL-related property controls for modem restart and init-time vendor properties are not defined in policy. This is the most likely cause of modem restart failures.
  • Penny device in PERMISSIVE mode masks severity: Penny's 3,600 denies are all permissive=1 (logged but not blocked). Switching to enforcing will cause widespread functional failures on this device.
